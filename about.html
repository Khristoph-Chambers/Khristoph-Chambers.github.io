ABOUT




Internet Exchange Points are places at which local Internet Service Providers exchange Internet traffic between their networks and are critical to the overall health of the Internet. These networks are uniquely described as Autonomous Systems and are identified by autonomous system numbers (ASNs) and IXPs typically require that entities seeking to participate at the IXP possess such a number before joining.


A local IXP allows ISPs to connect directly, locally and offers the possibility of exchanging traffic locally at no cost without the need to buy transit from other ISPs that may involve the use of costly foreign exchange.

The reason for this is that by peering at the IXP, participating ISPs now have the opportunity to be able to deliver local content locally rather than having to deliver this content to their upstream providers and subsequently are able to reduce the average per-bit delivery cost of their service as well as improve their profitability, reinvestment as well as their competiveness.
Further the opportunity for local exchange of traffic and routes improves the overall resilience of the fibre network as well as it offers the chance for improved latency for locally hosted content.

The IXP is its simplest sense is a layer 2 physical network switch and is usually operated by a single entity that often administers the IXP on a not-for-profit basis through an association of its participants. The arrangement involves the use of a shared switch fabric where participants typically arrange for peering through bi-lateral agreements for the exchange of traffic and routes through their respective routers.


BENEFITS

Benefits of Using Internet eXchange Point

Cost Reduction

By lowering Internet Service Provider (ISP) operational expenses and making Internet access more accessible to more local Internet users, IXPs can assist in lowering the cost of Internet access for end-users.

Latency and Bandwith Improvements

IXPs can boost bandwidth and lower latency by giving internet traffic a more direct path.

Increase Routing Efficiency and Fault Tolerance

By offering various pathways for Internet traffic, IXPs can aid in enhancing routing effectiveness and fault tolerance.





Sustainability

IXPs offer scalability for network growth and expansion. As your network demands increase, you can easily establish new peering relationships and exchange traffic with additional networks present at the exchange point, without requiring significant infrastructure upgrades.

Localized Traffic Exchange

Connecting to an IXp in your region allows for localized traffic exchange, reducing the need for long-distance connections and minimizing network congestion.

Redundancy

IXPs have multiple switches and routers that are connected to each other in a mesh topology. This topology ensures that if one switch or router fails, traffic can be rerouted through another switch or router. This is to ensure that all your data is always readily available.

THE JAMAICAN JOURNEY



The GOJ as part of its policy ICT policy made it a key priority that the nation should have island wide ICT infrastructure that facilitates greater access to high capacity networks with interconnection to international networks. They further went on state that as a part of the strategies to accomplish this was to establish a National Internet Exchange Point in Jamaica.


In addition to this there were key national initiatives underway, including the establishment of a Jamaican logistics hub, which would be helped by the presence of a local IXP that will be able to handle greater volumes of data that would be generated within the island as a result of these initiatives.

With the strategic objectives of the GOJ in mind, the Office of Utilities Regulation (OUR) set about the task of bringing key stakeholders together with the hosting of the Jamaican Internet Exchange Point Symposium in collaboration with the Caribbean Telecommunications Union and Packet Clearing House, the worldâ€™s leading implementer of Internet Exchange Points.


The symposium brought together members of academia, Internet Service Providers (ISPs), content houses and other interested parties. From the symposium the OUR, as facilitator of the IXP implementation process, was able to assemble a working group drawn from the gathering to establish the facility and move the process forward.


Through the efforts of the working group, the establishment of the IXP moved forward and a venue was selected for the hosting of the IXP. The site selected was eGov Jamaica Ltd and where the IXP is presently housed.






What is Peering?

Peering in an Internet Exchange Point (IXP) refers to the process of interconnecting networks and exchanging Internet traffic between different Internet Service Providers (ISPs), content delivery networks (CDNs), and other network operators. It enables efficient and direct routing of traffic between these networks, improving network performance, reducing latency, and lowering costs.
